N.J.A.C. 7:8 Stormwater Management Design Requirements

Stormwater Design Requirements
  • Stormwater Management Plan
    • Must include structural and non-structural measures (Page 12, "A stormwater management plan shall include structural and nonstructural...")
    • Must incorporate safety standards for stormwater basins (Page 12, "...shall incorporate the safety standards...")
    • Must consider ecological and physical site conditions (Page 12, "...shall consider the physical characteristics and ecological resources...")
    • Must be coordinated with other stormwater plans (Page 12, "...shall be coordinated with any other stormwater management plans...")
  • Groundwater Recharge
    • Must maintain 100% of average annual pre-development recharge volume (Page 28, "...shall be designed to maintain 100 percent of the average annual pre-construction groundwater recharge...")
  • Runoff Quantity Control
    • Must reduce post-construction peak runoff for 2-, 10-, and 100-year storms (Page 29, "...shall be designed to reduce the post-construction peak flow rates... 50, 75, and 80 percent...")
  • Runoff Quality Standards
    • Must remove 80% of TSS from post-construction runoff (Page 30, "...shall be designed to reduce the post-construction load of total suspended solids (TSS) by 80 percent...")
  • Non-structural Strategies
    • Must evaluate non-structural strategies before structural BMPs are used (Page 27, "...the development shall incorporate nonstructural stormwater management strategies to the maximum extent practicable...")
  • Maintenance Requirements
    • A maintenance plan is required for all stormwater measures (Page 32, "The design engineer shall prepare a maintenance plan for the stormwater management measures incorporated into the design of a major development...")
